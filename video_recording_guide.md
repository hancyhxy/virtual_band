# 30-Second Demo Video Recording Guide
## Interactive Visual Drum Kit - Video Production

This guide helps you create the perfect 30-second demo video for **Slide 1** of your presentation.

---

## VIDEO REQUIREMENTS

### Technical Specs:
- **Duration:** 30 seconds (28-32 seconds acceptable)
- **Resolution:** 1920Ã—1080 (Full HD) minimum
- **Frame Rate:** 30 FPS minimum (60 FPS preferred for smooth motion)
- **Format:** MP4, MOV, or M4V (Keynote compatible)
- **Audio:** Include audio! (drum sounds + optional background music)
- **File Size:** Under 50 MB for easy embedding

### Content Requirements:
- Show the full user experience in 30 seconds
- Demonstrate all 4 drums being played
- Highlight visual effects (particles, color shifts, glitch)
- Make it engaging and energetic
- No narration needed (let visuals speak)

---

## RECORDING SETUP

### Equipment:
1. **Screen Recording Software:**
   - **Mac:** QuickTime Player (built-in) or OBS Studio (free)
   - **Windows:** OBS Studio (free) or Xbox Game Bar (built-in)
   - **Online:** Loom (free tier works)

2. **Webcam:** Ensure good lighting on your hands
3. **Microphone:** Record system audio (drum sounds)
4. **Good lighting:** Bright, even lighting on you

### Environment Prep:
- **Clean background** behind you (minimal distractions)
- **Close other apps** to avoid notifications
- **Browser full-screen mode** (F11 or `Cmd + Ctrl + F`)
- **Hide cursor** (if possible in recording software)

### Before Recording:
1. Open your project in browser
2. Grant webcam/microphone permissions
3. Test that hand tracking is working smoothly
4. Practice your drumming sequence 2-3 times
5. Set particle style to your favorite (recommend `1` = Burst)

## 30-SECOND STORYBOARD

### Shot-by-Shot Timeline:

**0:00 - 0:03 (3 seconds): Introduction**
- **Show:** Calm state, hands entering frame
- **Action:**
  - Webcam feed visible with your face/hands
  - Skeletal tracking appears on your hands
  - All 4 drums clearly visible
  - Cool color palette (teal/indigo)
- **Visual:** Slow, deliberate hand movement
- **Audio:** Ambient quiet, maybe subtle background tone

**0:03 - 0:08 (5 seconds): First Exploration**
- **Show:** Hit each drum once slowly
- **Action:**

  - Snare (left-mid) â†’ SNAP

  - Each drum highlights when hit

- **Audio:** Clear drum sounds, distinct tones

**0:08 - 0:15 (7 seconds): Build the Rhythm**
- **Show:** Play a simple rhythm pattern
- **Action:**
  - Kick-Kick-Snare-Kick pattern
  - Or: Kick-Snare-Kick-Snare alternating
  - Use one or both hands
- **Visual:**
  - Particles accumulating
  - Background pixelation increasing
  - Colors shifting toward warm pink/orange
  - Faster hand movements
- **Audio:** Rhythmic drumming, building energy

**0:15 - 0:23 (8 seconds): Peak Intensity**
- **Show:** Rapid drumming, full visual effects
- **Action:**
  - Fast drum hits (aim for 4-6 hits in this section)
  - Include at least one Hi-Hat hit to trigger glitch
  - Use both hands if possible
- **Visual:**
  - Maximum particle density
  - Heavy pixelation (22px blocks)
  - Warm color palette dominant
  - Hi-Hat glitch effect (horizontal bands)
  - Saturation and hue rotation visible
- **Audio:** Energetic drumming, layered sounds

**0:23 - 0:30 (7 seconds): Showcase & Finish**
- **Show:** Final flourish, then gradual calm
- **Action:**
  - Last 2-3 dramatic hits
  - Let hands slow down and exit frame OR freeze
  - Effects decay naturally
- **Visual:**
  - Particles fading out
  - Colors cooling back to teal
  - Pixelation reducing
  - Elegant ending (not abrupt cut)
- **Audio:** Final drum hits echo, ambient fade

## PERFORMANCE TIPS

### Drumming Technique:
- **Start slow, build fast** - Creates narrative arc
- **Vary your rhythm** - Don't just hit randomly, create patterns
- **Hit all 4 drums** - Show variety
- **Include Hi-Hat hits** - Showcase the glitch effect (most impressive)
- **Use both hands** - Demonstrates multi-hand tracking
- **Big gestures** - Exaggerated "punches" look better on camera

### Visual Optimization:
- **Maximize browser window** - Fill entire screen
- **Good hand visibility** - Keep hands in well-lit area
- **Smooth movements** - Avoid jerky, unpredictable motions
- **Face optional** - You can crop to just hands + canvas if preferred

### Audio Quality:
- **Turn up volume** - Drum sounds should be clearly audible
- **Minimize background noise** - Close windows, turn off fans
- **Optional background music** - Add subtle music in editing (not required)

**Software Options:**

**Quick Edits:**

2. **Color correction** - Boost saturation, increase brightness
3. **Add title** - First 2 seconds: "Interactive Visual Drum Kit" fade-in text
4. **Sound mix** - Balance drum audio, add music if desired
5. **Transitions** - Smooth fade-in at start, fade-out at end
6. **Export:** 1920Ã—1080, 30 FPS, MP4

**Advanced Edits (if time allows):**
- **Split-screen:** Show your face + canvas simultaneously
- **Slow-motion:** Highlight a specific drum hit (0.5Ã— speed for 2-3 seconds)
- **Captions:** Label drums as you hit them ("Kick", "Snare", etc.)
- **Background music:** Subtle electronic/ambient track (royalty-free from YouTube Audio Library)

### Audio Enhancements:

If drum sounds are too quiet:
1. Import video into editor
2. Increase audio gain by +6 to +12 dB
3. Optional: Add light compression to even out volume
4. Export

## ALTERNATIVE: LIVE DEMO

If you prefer NOT to pre-record a video:

### Live Demo During Presentation:
**Pros:**
- More impressive (real-time interaction)
- Can respond to audience questions
- Shows confidence in your project

**Cons:**

- Hard to control timing (might go over 5 minutes)

3. Keep it short (30-60 seconds)

**Recommendation:** Pre-record a video for safety, but offer live demo during Q&A if tutor is interested.

## TROUBLESHOOTING

### Problem: Hand tracking is jittery in video
**Solution:**
- Ensure good lighting
- Use faster webcam (720p @ 60 FPS if possible)
- Reduce MediaPipe complexity (if configurable)

### Problem: Video is too large (>50 MB)
**Solution:**
- Compress using HandBrake (free)
- Reduce resolution to 1280Ã—720
- Lower bitrate to 5-8 Mbps

### Problem: Drum sounds aren't recording
**Solution:**
- Check recording software captures "System Audio"
- Increase browser volume before recording
- Test with a short 5-second recording first

### Problem: Colors look washed out in recording
**Solution:**
- Increase saturation in post-production
- Boost contrast
- Ensure browser is using hardware acceleration
